## Mechanism before metaphor

A cabinet begins with observable state, hidden state, actions, transition rules, score, and terminal conditions. Story, artwork, and sound are presentation layers; they cannot change those rules.

## Fidelity labels

- **Paper-derived:** the mechanic is linked to identified papers. The source panel distinguishes sourced behavior from abstraction and invented balancing.
- **Cryptography Classic:** a generic teaching cabinet, not a claim about Karim's research.
- **Security or experimental:** an adjacent systems, networking, optimization, privacy, or AI-assurance game with an explicit maturity label.

## Maturity is not decoration

`Planned`, `research review`, `prototype`, and `released` mean different things. A stable catalog page can exist before a playable model; that lets sources and limits be reviewed before animation makes an idea look more authoritative than it is.

## Presentation bundles

The long-term design supports Paper Lab, Classic Cabinet, Ink Circuit, Neon Terminal, Techno-Noir City, Command Grid, and Xenocrypt. These are original presentation systems inspired by broad genres. They do not copy franchise characters, symbols, distinctive interfaces, music, or trade dress. Inspect the shipped token and licensing contracts in the [theme specimen gallery]({{ '/arcade/themes/' | relative_url }}).

## Credits and licenses

Each released cabinet will list research authors, game design and implementation, editorial review, art, audio, font, and license credits. The initial registry and platform implementation are documented in the [master plan]({{ '/docs/cryptography-arcade-master-plan.html' | relative_url }}).
