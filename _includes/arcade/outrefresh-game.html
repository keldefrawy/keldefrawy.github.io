Rules, assumptions, and source disclaimer

This is an illustrative hybrid, not a protocol simulator. The mobile adversary moves only along declared network edges and retains distinct current-epoch shares. A successful refresh advances the epoch and assumes obsolete honest state is securely erased; old and new exposures do not combine. Recovery takes selected parties offline for 2.2 seconds.

Privacy fails when four compatible shares are read in one epoch. Availability fails when fewer than four parties are online, but this does not cryptographically erase the secret. Real proactive secret sharing and MPC require separate assumptions about detection, reboot, erasure, communication, correctness, robustness, and adversarial control.
